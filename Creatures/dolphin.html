Dolphin

This aquatic mammal is known to be very intelligent; dolphins have shown that they're able to teach, learn, cooperate, scheme, and grieve. They're also highly social animals, often living in pods of up to a dozen individuals (although this varies with species and location). In places with a high abundance of food, they're even known to form "superpods" of over 1000 dolphins. They can communicate with each other using a variety of clicks, whist-like sounds and other vocalizations. They're even known to use nonverbal communication by means of touch and posturing. Dolphins also display culture: some species teach their young to use tools and they exhibit other learned behaviors like using sponges to protect their mouths.
